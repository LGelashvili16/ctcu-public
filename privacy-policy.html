The Lightning Network can process payments quickly and cheaply by opening a "channel". A channel enables two parties to send and receive Bitcoin from one another. You can do this as many times as you want, and when you're done, you can decide to close the channel. This will update the main Bitcoin blockchain with the correct balances of both parties and record everything as just a single transaction. The best thing about these channels is that they work much like a large network. If you want to send bitcoin to someone but don't have a channel open, you can send the bitcoin and it will go through existing channels until it reaches its destination. The more channels that are open, the easier it becomes to transact with people.
